Discrepancies in public data publications of the EU states

 

The openness of data has increased in importance in recent years. For example, in 2019, the old EU Open Data Directive of 2003 was just updated to improve the openness of data. The directive also addresses data quality, but tends not to put it on an equal footing with openness, as the focus is clearly on improving openness.
But also the coverage of the published data is a necessary requirement, because without qualitative data, the best openness is useless. What is openness if there is no data that can be open? While everyone is free to use the data, the problems cannot be solved without sufficient data.
This interactive site shows the gaps in the openness of EU countries. For this, the ODIN Score collected by Open Data Watch is visualized. The goal of Open Data Watch is "to help countries build better and more resilient national statistical systems". The Odin Score evaluates the governmental statistical publications and rates each country with a score between 0 and 100. To calculate the score, it is divided into openness and coverage. The openness refers to the accessibility of the data, while the coverage refers to the amount and detailedness of the data.
